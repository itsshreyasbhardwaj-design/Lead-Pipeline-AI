# Examples Overview

Six fully worked examples live in `/examples`, one per industry, each showing a complete filled-out Company Context plus real pipeline outputs (research, qualification, outreach, LinkedIn) for a fictional target prospect. Use these as calibration for what "good" looks like, and as starting points if your business is close to one of these verticals.

| Industry | Folder | What it demonstrates |
|---|---|---|
| B2B SaaS | `examples/saas/` | Technical buyer, product-led signal-based outreach (the reference example used throughout `/prompts` and `/docs`) |
| Marketing / Creative Agencies | `examples/agencies/` | Relationship-driven, portfolio-proof-driven outreach; longer sales cycle, multiple stakeholders |
| AI Startups | `examples/ai-startups/` | Fast-moving, technically sophisticated buyers; credibility-first messaging |
| Education | `examples/education/` | Institutional buyers (schools, consultants), parent/student end-user dynamic, seasonal buying triggers |
| Healthcare | `examples/healthcare/` | Compliance-sensitive buyers, longer trust-building cycle, risk-averse objection patterns |
| Manufacturing | `examples/manufacturing/` | Traditional/offline-influenced buyers, ROI/efficiency-first framing, longer B2B procurement cycles |

## How to use these examples

1. Find the closest match to your business, or read all six for range if your business doesn't map cleanly to one.
2. Compare the specificity level in their Company Context against your own draft — this is the fastest way to spot where your own file is too generic.
3. Compare their outreach email against the "swap test" described in `docs/prompt-guide.md` — notice how each is written so it could only be sent to that one fictional prospect.
4. Do not copy the proof points, case studies, or specific numbers — they're fictional and exist to demonstrate structure, not to be reused as your own claims.

## A note on fictional data

Every company, contact name, and statistic in `/examples` is fictional or composited, clearly for demonstration purposes. No real companies, executives, or private data are represented. If you're contributing a new example per `CONTRIBUTING.md`, this same rule applies — fictional composites only.
