# Example: Healthcare

All names, companies, and statistics below are fictional composites for demonstration purposes.

## Company Context

```markdown
## Company Overview
Company Name: Verdant Health Compliance
One-line description: Compliance automation platform for outpatient clinics
managing HIPAA audit prep and staff training tracking.
Founded / Stage: Series A, 32 employees

## What We Sell
Product: Verdant Compliance Suite
Pricing model: Annual subscription, tiered by clinic size/location count
Typical deal size: $12K–$45K ACV

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Segment: Multi-location outpatient clinic groups (dental, dermatology,
physical therapy, urgent care)
Company size: 3–30 locations, 50–500 staff
Geography: US
Buyer roles who initiate: Compliance Officer, Practice Manager
Buyer roles who approve budget: COO, Managing Partner

## What We Solve
1. Manual HIPAA audit prep consumes weeks of staff time before every review
2. Staff training compliance tracked in spreadsheets, hard to prove at audit time
3. Multi-location groups lack a single source of truth across sites
Cost of inaction: Audit findings, potential fines, reputational risk, wasted
staff hours during every audit cycle
Why alternatives fall short: Generic compliance software isn't healthcare-specific;
spreadsheets don't scale past a handful of locations

## Unique Advantages
Healthcare-specific audit templates built with former compliance officers
Proof point: Reduced audit prep time from 3 weeks to 4 days for a 12-location
dermatology group (fictional, illustrative)
Competitors: Generic GRC software, manual/spreadsheet processes

## Brand Voice
3 words we are: Trustworthy, precise, calm
3 words we are not: Alarmist, salesy, generic
Tone: Risk-aware but not fear-based — evidence and process, not scare tactics

## Buying Triggers
1. Recent expansion (new location opened — verified via press/local news)
2. Recent audit finding or near-miss (harder to verify directly, often surfaces
   in conversation, not before)
3. New Compliance Officer or Practice Manager hire

## Success Metrics
Discovery calls booked, pilot-to-signed conversion

## Objections We Hear Most Often
1. "We handle this in-house fine" — reframe: ask how audit prep time has
   trended as locations were added; in-house processes rarely scale linearly
2. "Sensitive about a new vendor touching compliance data" — reframe: detail
   security posture and offer to start with a single location as a pilot
3. "Budget already allocated for the year" — reframe: offer to begin a pilot
   scoped to fit remaining budget, or a start date aligned to next fiscal year

## Social Proof
Customers: One 12-location dermatology group, one 8-location physical therapy
group (fictional, named with permission in real usage)
Quantified results: Audit prep time reduced from 3 weeks to 4 days
```

## Target Prospect: Meridian Dermatology Partners (fictional)

14-location dermatology group across two states. Opened 3 new locations in
the past 8 months (verified via press releases and location pages on their
website). No confirmed name for a Compliance Officer — practice website
lists a "Director of Operations" who may hold this responsibility.

**Qualification:** 47/70 — Nurture (borderline Pursue). Need strong (rapid
expansion typically strains manual compliance processes); Authority weak
(no confirmed compliance-specific contact identified yet).

```

Would it be useful to have a short call about how compliance tracking is
holding up across the newer locations?

[Name]
```
