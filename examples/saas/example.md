# Example: B2B SaaS

All names, companies, and statistics below are fictional composites for demonstration purposes.

## Company Context

```markdown
## Company Overview
Company Name: Northbeam Observability
One-line description: DevOps observability platform that reduces alert noise
and cuts incident resolution time for engineering teams running Kubernetes.
Website: northbeam.example
Founded / Stage: Series A, 45 employees
Team size: 45

## What We Sell
Product: Northbeam Platform (observability + intelligent alert routing)
Pricing model: Usage-based (per host/node), annual contracts standard at scale
Typical deal size: $30K–$150K ACV

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Industry: Technology companies running production Kubernetes workloads
Company size: 100–1,000 employees
Geography: US, UK
Stage: Series B+ or profitable/bootstrapped equivalent scale
Technographic signals: Kubernetes, active migration from legacy infra (ECS/VMs)
Buyer roles who initiate: Platform Engineering Lead, SRE Lead
Buyer roles who approve budget: VP Engineering, CTO

## What We Solve
1. Alert fatigue during infrastructure migrations — noise spikes 3-4x
2. Slow incident resolution due to fragmented tooling across old and new stack
3. On-call burnout driving engineering attrition
Cost of inaction: Prolonged MTTR, engineer attrition, delayed migration timelines
Why alternatives fall short: Legacy APM tools weren't built for dynamic K8s
environments; generic dashboards add tools instead of reducing noise

## Unique Advantages
Integrates directly with existing APM stack rather than replacing it
Proof point: 40% average reduction in alert-to-resolution time in first 90 days
Competitors: Datadog (broader, less migration-specific), Grafana (self-hosted, high setup cost)

## Brand Voice
3 words we are: Direct, technical, credible
3 words we are not: Salesy, jargon-heavy, hype-driven
Tone: Peer-to-peer engineer tone, not marketing tone
Never say: "revolutionize," "game-changing," "synergy"

## Buying Triggers
1. Public Kubernetes/infra migration announcement (blog, conference talk)
2. Multiple "Platform Engineer" / "SRE" job postings in a short window
3. Recent funding round (signals scaling infra investment)

## Success Metrics
Meetings booked, demo-to-opportunity conversion, pipeline generated

## Objections We Hear Most Often
1. "We already have an APM tool" — reframe: we integrate with it, don't replace it
2. "Too early, we just started migrating" — reframe: earliest migration weeks are
   when alert noise is worst; earlier adoption prevents burnout before it starts
3. "No budget this quarter" — reframe: cost of unresolved alert fatigue (attrition,
   delayed migration) typically exceeds our price within one quarter

## Social Proof
Customers: Flexport-style logistics platforms, two Series C fintech companies
(named with permission in real usage — fictional here)
Quantified results: 40% reduction in alert-to-resolution time (avg, first 90 days)
```
