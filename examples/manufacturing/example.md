# Example: Manufacturing

All names, companies, and statistics below are fictional composites for demonstration purposes.

## Company Context

```markdown
## Company Overview
Company Name: Ironclad Predictive
One-line description: Predictive maintenance software for mid-size industrial
manufacturers, reducing unplanned downtime through sensor-based failure prediction.
Founded / Stage: Series B, 60 employees

## What We Sell
Product: Ironclad Predictive Maintenance Platform (hardware sensors + software)
Pricing model: Hardware install fee + annual software subscription per line
Typical deal size: $80K–$300K (install + first year)

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Industry: Discrete manufacturing (automotive parts, industrial equipment,
packaging machinery)
Company size: 200–2,000 employees, 1+ production facilities
Geography: US, with facilities in the Midwest/Southeast manufacturing corridors
Buyer roles who initiate: Plant Manager, Maintenance Manager
Buyer roles who approve budget: VP Operations, Plant Director

## What We Solve
1. Unplanned equipment downtime costs manufacturers an average of tens of
   thousands of dollars per incident in lost production
2. Reactive maintenance schedules waste labor on healthy equipment while
   missing failures on at-risk equipment
3. Aging equipment lacks modern monitoring without a full capital replacement
Cost of inaction: Continued unplanned downtime, higher total maintenance
labor cost, delayed order fulfillment
Why alternatives fall short: Full equipment replacement is capital-intensive;
generic IoT platforms lack manufacturing-specific failure models

## Unique Advantages
Retrofit sensor kits work on existing equipment — no capital replacement needed
Proof point: Reduced unplanned downtime 35% for a 400-employee auto parts
manufacturer within two quarters (fictional, illustrative)
Competitors: Full equipment replacement, generic IoT monitoring platforms

## Brand Voice
3 words we are: Practical, ROI-focused, plant-floor credible
3 words we are not: Abstract, tech-hype-driven, corporate-jargon-heavy
Tone: Speaks the language of plant operations, leads with concrete numbers

## Buying Triggers
1. Recent public incident/recall tied to equipment failure (industry-wide,
   not company-specific — raises category awareness)
2. New Plant Manager or VP Operations hire (often re-evaluates maintenance strategy)
3. Facility expansion or new production line announcement

## Success Metrics
Plant visits/site assessments booked, pilot line conversions

## Objections We Hear Most Often
1. "Our equipment is too old for this" — reframe: retrofit kits are built
   specifically for legacy equipment, no replacement required
2. "We already do preventive maintenance on a schedule" — reframe: scheduled
   maintenance still misses unpredictable failures; predictive catches what
   schedules can't
3. "Long capital approval process" — reframe: offer a single-line pilot
   scoped below typical capital approval thresholds

## Social Proof
Customers: One 400-employee auto parts manufacturer, one packaging equipment
manufacturer (fictional, named with permission in real usage)
Quantified results: 35% reduction in unplanned downtime within two quarters
```
