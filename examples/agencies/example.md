# Example: Marketing / Creative Agency

All names, companies, and statistics below are fictional composites for demonstration purposes.

## Company Context

```markdown
## Company Overview
Company Name: Fieldstone Creative
One-line description: Full-service brand and performance marketing agency for
DTC consumer brands scaling past $5M revenue.
Founded / Stage: 12 years, independent, 28 employees

## What We Sell
Services: Brand strategy, paid media management, creative production
Pricing model: Monthly retainer + project fees
Typical deal size: $8K–$25K/month retainer

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Industry: DTC consumer brands (beauty, wellness, home goods)
Company size: $5M–$50M revenue, 15–150 employees
Geography: US
Stage: Post-product-market-fit, scaling paid acquisition
Buyer roles who initiate: Head of Marketing, Growth Lead
Buyer roles who approve budget: CMO, CEO/Founder (at smaller end)

## What We Solve
1. In-house creative team can't keep pace with paid media testing volume
2. Agencies they've used before optimize for billable hours, not performance
3. Brand consistency breaks down as they scale multiple channels
Cost of inaction: Rising CAC from creative fatigue, inconsistent brand equity
Why alternatives fall short: Big agencies are slow and generic; freelancers
lack integrated strategy-to-production pipeline

## Unique Advantages
In-house production studio — no outsourced creative, faster iteration
Proof point: Reduced CAC 22% for a home goods brand within one quarter via
creative testing velocity increase
Competitors: Large holding-company agencies (slow), freelance networks (inconsistent)

## Brand Voice
3 words we are: Sharp, collaborative, performance-obsessed
3 words we are not: Corporate, slow, precious-about-creative
Tone: Confident but not arrogant; speaks in outcomes, not "big idea" jargon

## Buying Triggers
1. New funding round or notable revenue milestone announcement
2. Recent CMO/Head of Marketing hire (new leader often re-evaluates agencies)
3. Visible high ad spend with declining creative variety (observable via ad libraries)

## Success Metrics
Discovery calls booked, proposal-to-signed conversion

## Objections We Hear Most Often
1. "We have an in-house team" — reframe: we extend capacity for testing
   volume, don't replace strategic ownership
2. "Bad experience with agencies before" — reframe: ask what specifically
   went wrong, reference our production-in-house model as the structural fix
3. "Retainer feels expensive" — reframe: compare to fully-loaded cost of
   equivalent in-house hires plus tooling

## Social Proof
Customers: Two funded DTC beauty brands, one home goods brand (fictional, named
with permission in real usage)
Quantified results: 22% CAC reduction in one quarter (home goods brand)
```
