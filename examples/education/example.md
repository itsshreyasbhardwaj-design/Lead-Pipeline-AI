# Example: Education

All names, companies, and statistics below are fictional composites for demonstration purposes.

## Company Context

```markdown
## Company Overview
Company Name: Northstar Admissions AI
One-line description: AI-powered admissions counseling platform helping
students build stronger university applications through personalized,
evidence-based guidance.
Primary Market: India
Secondary Markets: Middle East, Southeast Asia, UK, USA

## What We Sell
Product: AI Admissions Counsellor platform + school partnership program
Pricing model: Direct-to-consumer subscription; B2B2C school licensing
Typical deal size (school partnerships): $5K–$40K/year depending on student count

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) — B2B Partnership Track
Segment: International/IB schools, education consultants, coaching institutes
Geography: India, UAE, Singapore
Buyer roles who initiate: School Counselor, Director of College Guidance
Buyer roles who approve budget: School Principal, School Director

## What We Solve
1. Schools can't offer 1:1 admissions counseling depth at scale with limited staff
2. Students apply to universities without a data-driven strategy, wasting
   application fees on poor-fit schools
3. Parents lack visibility into whether their child's profile is competitive
Cost of inaction: Lower admit rates hurt school reputation and enrollment;
families spend thousands on external consultants schools can't offer in-house
Why alternatives fall short: Traditional consultants are expensive and don't
scale across a whole grade cohort; generic AI chatbots give generic advice

## Unique Advantages
Persistent AI memory of each student's full profile over multiple years
Proof point: Partner schools saw a measurable increase in top-30 university
placements after one full admissions cycle (fictional, illustrative)
Competitors: Crimson Education, Yocket, Leverage Edu, independent consultants

## Brand Voice
3 words we are: Premium, intelligent, supportive
3 words we are not: Pushy, salesy, generic
Tone: Educational and consultative — never pressure-based, always evidence-first

## Buying Triggers
1. Admissions season ramp-up (Grade 11 planning cycles)
2. School announces new counseling department investment or hire
3. Board exam results season (schools re-evaluate counseling support)

## Success Metrics
School partnership meetings booked, demo requests, parent consultation conversions

## Objections We Hear Most Often
1. "We already have a counseling department" — reframe: we augment counselor
   capacity, not replace it — one counselor can support far more students
2. "AI feels impersonal for something this important" — reframe: every
   recommendation is evidence-backed and reviewable by the human counselor;
   it's a force-multiplier, not a replacement for human judgment
3. "Budget cycle already set" — reframe: offer a pilot with a subset of one
   grade to de-risk timing

## Social Proof
Partner schools: Two international schools in the UAE, one IB school in India
(fictional, named with permission in real usage)
Quantified results: Measurable increase in top-tier placements after one cycle
```

update announcing a new "Head of University Guidance" hire (verified,

**Qualification:** 52/70 — Pursue Now. Timing strong (new leadership hire,
typically re-evaluates tools/vendors in first 90 days); Authority moderate
(new hire may need Principal sign-off for new spend).

**Outreach Email:**

