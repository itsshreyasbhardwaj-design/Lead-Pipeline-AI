# Outreach Framework

The structural framework behind `prompts/outreach-email.md` and `prompts/linkedin.md`, presented here as a standalone reference/checklist you can apply manually or hand to anyone writing outreach without the full prompt pipeline.

```markdown
## The Four-Part Structure

1. PERSONALIZED OPENING (1–2 sentences)
   - References a specific, verifiable fact about the prospect
   - Never restates something obvious/public ("I see you're the VP of X at Y")
   - Passes the "swap test": could NOT be sent to a different company unchanged

2. BRIDGE (1–2 sentences)
   - Connects the opening signal to a real pain point
   - The pain point should be one your Company Context explicitly claims to solve
   - Avoid: jumping straight from signal to pitch with no logical bridge

3. VALUE (1 sentence)
   - One specific proof point — a result, a mechanism, a named comparable customer
   - Avoid: generic claims ("we help companies like yours succeed")

4. CALL TO ACTION (1 sentence)
   - Exactly one ask
   - Low-friction, specific ("worth 15 minutes next week?" not "let me know your thoughts")

## Pre-Send Checklist
- [ ] Every fact is Verified or clearly labeled inference — none invented
- [ ] At least 3 genuine personalization points existed before writing (from research)
- [ ] Passes the swap test (not generic enough to send elsewhere unchanged)
- [ ] Single CTA only
- [ ] No banned filler phrases ("hope you're doing well," "just following up," etc.)
- [ ] Under 120 words (email) / 300 characters (LinkedIn connection note)
- [ ] Would a real senior AE at this company actually send this?
```
